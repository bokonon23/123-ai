What Will Be the Money of the Internet?

A conversation about the gap the existing financial system can't fill

Originally published on Blockstrata.co · Bruce Milligan

← Back to Blog
TL;DRThe current systems for conducting commerce online have not evolved to service the global and borderless nature of the internet. A global internet-native currency — based on cryptocurrency — would offer low transaction fees, decentralisation, and the ability to facilitate micropayments, cross-border payments, IoT transactions, and financial inclusion for the unbanked.

A Conversation Worth Having

For those of us who have been working in the blockchain world for some time, it's easy to get lost in the detail. Here's an attempt to explain the basics through a conversation with a friend who has barely any awareness of cryptocurrency — or why they should care.

Me: So what do you think will be the Money of the internet?

Friend: The internet already has money. I use PayPal, credit cards, bank transfers — there are a thousand payment service providers. What makes you think the golden age of commerce on the internet isn't already here?

Me: We recreated the high street online and made digital versions of old analogue bank services. But consider: every time you use a browser you see the familiar lock icon — indicating a site is secured by HTTPS. So far there has been no establishment of an internet standard for financial transactions. The underlying 'money' is still cumbersome and not fit for a truly borderless internet.

Why Micropayments Matter

Micropayments — transactions of less than a dollar, sometimes less than 1 cent — are not viable on the traditional financial system. Low transaction fees make crypto well-suited here. The ability to pay $0.01 to view a webpage, processed automatically from a wallet you own — received directly by the creator — could flip the advertising-centric model of the internet entirely.

The IoT and AI Connection

We have moved beyond it just being humans that need payment facilities. Machines will need means of payment and receipt for services — imagine a vending machine that reorders its own stock, or AI agents executing transactions on your behalf. In the traditional world, a human would need to sit behind the account. Not so with smart contracts and crypto payments.

Cross-Border Payments & Financial Inclusion

There is no reason to still be taking 3–5 days and $30 to send funds across borders. Instant payments with negligible transaction fees are the way of the future. Crypto also provides an alternative banking facility to unbanked and under-banked individuals — giving them access to new economic opportunities through a mobile device.

The Bull Case

At the root, all of humanity's achievements trace back to our ability for grand-scale coordination. What has been created with the advent of cryptocurrency protocols is a way of coordinating economic activity with tamper-proof ledgers that are not owned by anyone — a shield against corruption.

If a cryptocurrency protocol becomes widely adopted as the settlement layer for internet commerce, it could be indicated by its logo in the browser bar — similar to the way HTTPS shows a lock symbol. Imagine a world where this is reality, and it's hard not to get excited about the possibilities.
