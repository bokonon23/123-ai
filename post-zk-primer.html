Zero Knowledge: SNARKs, STARKs & Rollups

A basic primer for the uninitiated

Originally published on Blockstrata.co · Bruce Milligan

← Back to Blog
"ZK SNARKs are as important a technological breakthrough as blockchains themselves." — Vitalik Buterin, September 2022
TL;DRzk-proofs were the original mathematical innovation. zk-rollups can help batch transactions and scale blockchains. zk-SNARKs and zk-STARKs are both methods of implementing zk-rollups. Several projects are racing to scale blockchains — particularly Ethereum — with this technology. zkEVM is the latest development, allowing smart contracts to be included rather than just simple transactions.

What is Zero Knowledge?

"ZK" is shorthand for zk-proofs — a breakthrough mathematical approach to sharing information. A zk-proof is a method by which one party (the prover) can prove to another party (the verifier) that a given statement is true, while avoiding conveying any additional information apart from the fact that the statement is true.

A quick metaphor: imagine you're looking at a 'Where's Wally?' puzzle and want to prove to a friend you know where Wally is without revealing how to find him. You take a piece of card larger than the puzzle, cut out a hole the size of Wally, and place it over the picture — revealing only Wally. Your friend can see that you knew, without learning anything about his location.

Why Have zk-Proofs Become Important to Blockchain?

The first implementations were in privacy projects like Zcash. But privacy isn't the main reason for Vitalik's excitement. The big goal is scalability — zk-related tech is the prime candidate to deliver orders of magnitude of scalability to Ethereum.

zk-proofs can be interactive (secure but slow) or non-interactive (a single exchange of information, enabling the speed that makes this technology transformational). With non-interactive proofs, there is a greater requirement to perform proving calculations — the 'Prover' and decentralising it is where most engineering challenges are focused.

zk-SNARKs vs. zk-STARKs

SNARK — Succinct Non-Interactive ARguments of Knowledge: compact proof size, quick to verify, single-transaction generation. Zcash has used SNARKs since 2016.

STARK — Scalable Transparent ARgument of Knowledge: enables off-chain computation for exponential scalability, and uses publicly-available randomness — eliminating the need for a trusted setup that SNARKs require.

Both approaches allow for fast verification and less block space, meaning more scalability for networks like Ethereum.

zk-Rollups

zk-Rollups increase scalability by bundling thousands of transactions in a batch and posting only minimal summary data to the base layer. This is where the real excitement lies — not just privacy, but dramatically increasing the number of transactions Ethereum can process per second.

zkEVM — The Latest Development

zkEVM recreates existing EVM opcodes for proving and verification in circuits, allowing them to execute smart contracts — the best of both worlds. Projects like StarkNet, ZKSync, Polygon Zero, and SCROLL were racing to build this in 2022, and significant progress has been made since.

Why This Still Matters

ZK technology is now at the heart of AI privacy, verifiable computation, and identity systems. Steve Newcomb of ZKSync described it well: "I liken it to when nobody trusted their credit cards on the browser and then we agreed on SSL everywhere — we got the lock icon and suddenly people started using their credit cards online. It created e-commerce. I think that's going to be the same thing with zkEVM rollup tech."
