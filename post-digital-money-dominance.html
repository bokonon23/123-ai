The Race for Digital Money Dominance

Stablecoins, CBDCs, and the self-custodied future

Originally published on Blockstrata.co · Bruce Milligan

← Back to Blog

Digital money represents a transformative shift in how we perceive and use currency. Unlike traditional forms tied to physical assets and government backing, digital money exists purely in electronic form — driven by advancements in blockchain technology.

A noteworthy aspect of the current landscape: approximately 99% of stablecoin volume is denominated in dollars, effectively solidifying the US dollar's status as the de facto digital currency for global transactions.

The Landscape of Digital Money

Cryptocurrencies are decentralised digital assets built on blockchain technology, allowing peer-to-peer transactions without central oversight. Bitcoin, Ethereum, and countless altcoins exemplify this category.

Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) are digital currencies backed by government entities. Countries like China, Sweden, and the Bahamas have already begun piloting or implementing their CBDCs.

The Case for Self-Custodied Money

Self-custodied money refers to digital currency that individuals manage and control directly, without relying on intermediaries such as banks or payment processors.

  • Autonomy: Direct control over funds, eliminating third-party restrictions or delays
  • Privacy: Facilitates anonymous exchanges, protecting users from the surveillance often associated with traditional banking
  • Speed: Enables instant transactions — increasingly vital for AI-powered applications and real-time automation

The Role of AI in Digital Transactions

Smart contracts are self-executing contracts with terms written directly into code, automating various processes. In a real estate transaction, a smart contract could manage the transfer of funds and ownership once predefined conditions are met.

AI agents can analyse market conditions, assess risks, and make decisions on behalf of users — autonomously buying or selling assets using self-custodied funds. This isn't science fiction; it's already happening.

Cryptocurrency vs. CBDCs

Cryptocurrencies offer decentralisation and censorship resistance, encouraging rapid innovation. But they face regulatory hurdles and trust issues that complicate mainstream acceptance.

CBDCs face their own challenges around implementation infrastructure, cybersecurity, and public trust concerns around surveillance and data misuse.

Predictions for the Evolving Landscape

The most likely future is coexistence — users choosing between self-custodied crypto for autonomy and CBDCs for everyday stability. This diversity could create a more resilient financial system overall.

Self-custodied digital money is poised to emerge as a dominant force — particularly as AI-to-AI transactions become an increasingly real use case for borderless, programmable money. The businesses that understand this early will have a significant advantage.
